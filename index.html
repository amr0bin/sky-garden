A garden in the sky

Spéirghairdín — from spéir, sky, and gairdín, garden

My balcony — twenty feet by five, grown entirely in pots and tended season by season. I've gathered these plants to carry a thread of my Irish and wider Celtic heritage into a Vancouver sky: for gentle medicine, for the birds and bees who visit, and for the quiet work of making a home far from home. I explore the garden plan to meet each plant, and the harvest calendar to see what to gather and make through the year.

I click any month to see what's ready then — the dotted month is today's, and arrow keys step through them. A rough guide for a mild coastal climate; I tap a plant for the detail. Foxglove is grown for lore and pollinators only, so it carries no harvest.

A note to myself on safety. These are traditional and herbal uses I've gathered for interest and heritage — not medical advice. I identify carefully, I remember that several of these plants have real cautions or interactions, and that foxglove is here for lore and the bees only and must never be taken. Before I prepare anything for myself or a guest, I cross-check a trusted herbal reference or a qualified practitioner, and I patch-test any topical first.

A note to myself on heritage. I've drawn the Irish (Gaeilge) names and folklore from Irish plant-lore sources, and kept them only where I could reasonably verify them; where I haven't yet found a confident name, I've left it open. This is a living record — I'll correct and add to it as my garden and my knowledge grow.
